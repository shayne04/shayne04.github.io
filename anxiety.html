Managing Anxiety 101

Definitions, Causes and Coping By Resolve Psychology June 6, 2024

What is Anxiety?

Anxiety is worry, nervousness, dread or fear about something that might happen in the immediate or distant future. While occasional anxiety is a normal part of human life, when it becomes chronic or interferes with you living your life in the way you wish, it could cross over into what is understood as an anxiety disorder. According to the Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders (DSM-5), which is the reference and authoritative guide for diagnosing mental health conditions, anxiety disorders are characterized by chronic, excessive anxiety that interferes with daily life. Anxiety disorders refers to a group of mental health conditions in which anxiety is the primary feature but the perceived threat itself varies.

Causes of Anxiety

Psychological research has identified that anxiety is a complex condition influenced by multiple factors. Understanding these risk factors and possible causes can help in developing effective treatments and coping strategies for anxiety disorders. Amongst the most common causes are:

Genetic Factors

Scientific evidence indicates that a family history of anxiety disorders can increase the probability of developing anxiety. Genetic predisposition plays a vital role in vulnerability to anxiety.

Brain Chemistry and Circuitry

Imbalances in neurotransmitters — chemicals that transmit signals in the brain — can contribute to anxiety and other mental disorders.

Childhood Experiences

Overly critical, neglectful, or emotionally unavailable parenting can increase the chances of developing anxiety later in life.

Life Experiences

Trauma, abuse, grief, or prolonged stress can trigger anxiety disorders, especially for those with a genetic predisposition.

Environmental Stressors

Climate change, discrimination, high-pressure environments, and major life transitions can all contribute to anxiety.

Medical Factors

Certain medications, drug use, and health conditions like hyperthyroidism, vitamin imbalances, or hormonal changes are linked to anxiety.

Effective Coping Strategies

Managing anxiety involves a combination of self-care practices, therapy which teaches coping skills, and, in some cases, medication. Here are some effective strategies:

Psychological Therapy (Psychotherapy)

Multiple psychotherapeutic approaches can help the person deal with the anxiety disorder. However, cognitive-behavioural therapy (CBT) is particularly effective in treating anxiety disorders. It allows individuals to identify and challenge negative thought patterns as well as behaviours that can help manage anxiety in the short-term but contribute to the maintenance of long-term anxiety. Also, it helps clients identify unhelpful thoughts and replace them with more helpful and balanced ones. Therapy can also help clients work through situations that trigger anxiety.

Relaxation techniques

Relaxation techniques can take a number of forms. This includes deep breathing, visualization, mindfulness, meditation, and yoga amongst other relaxation exercises. Regular practice of relaxation exercises can reduce anxiety by promoting deep relaxation and promoting physiological changes associated with a state of calm. Regular, daily, practice of deep relaxation can also generalize to times outside of the practice and can contribute to better management of anxiety overall.

Regular exercise

Scientific studies consistently highlight regular exercise’s natural and holistic benefits for physical and mental health. Physical activity triggers the release of endorphins, which naturally improve mood and provide a sense of muscle relaxation.

Engaging in regular exercise can also serve as a healthy distraction from anxious and ruminative thoughts, offering a sense of control and positive outlook on anxiety management. Some types of physical activity that have been found helpful include brisk walking, running, cycling, swimming, aerobic classes, and walking. Exercising in green spaces has the added benefit of allowing people to focus on their senses and shift their attention away from anxious thoughts.

Healthy lifestyle

Physical health specialists talk about the importance of maintaining a balanced diet, getting enough sleep and avoiding or reducing caffeine and alcohol intake, all of which can reduce anxiety symptoms. Additionally, avoiding or minimizing the consumption of substances that can alter the nervous system can support a person in managing anxiety.

Support network

Establishing and nurturing a support network is crucial for all humans, including individuals experiencing anxiety. A support network, which can include family, friends, or support groups, provides a sense of community and reduces feelings of isolation. Engaging in interactive activities with this support circle, such as conversations or group activities, can contribute to alleviating anxiety symptoms, making a person feel understood, experience a sense of belonging, and less alone in their journey.
